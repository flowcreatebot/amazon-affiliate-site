## Actionable structure patterns

### 1) Title angle patterns that recur
- Dominant formula: `Best Espresso Machines Under $200 (2026)` + list count.
- High-performing variants add proof tokens: `tested`, `expert tested`, `reviews`, `updated`.
- CTR hooks frequently include beginner framing (`for beginners`, `easy to use`) or value framing (`budget`, `best value`).

**Template action:** keep exact price-qualified keyword in first 60 chars + current year + one proof token.

### 2) Section order pattern (dominant)
1. Short intro hook (budget pain point + “real espresso at home” promise)
2. Ranked product list (often starts immediately, minimal preamble)
3. Buyer criteria / buying guide factors (pressure, milk frothing, cleanup)
4. FAQ or embedded Q&A
5. Closing recommendation

**Template action:** keep quick-scan blocks above the fold (winner box + comparison table) before long reviews.

### 3) Comparison table style pattern
Strong pages include a comparison table or quick-spec matrix with these recurring dimensions:
- Product/model
- Best for
- Machine type (semi-auto/manual/pod)
- Pressure claim (15/20 bar)
- Milk frother availability/type
- Water tank size
- Price band

**Template action:** enforce espresso-specific columns (machine type, portafilter/basket style, heat-up time, milk system, cup clearance).

### 4) Product review block pattern
Most pages repeat this structure per product:
- “Best for” positioning line
- Pros / cons bullets
- Key specs snapshot
- Verdict with target user fit

**Template action:** force one-line *who should buy / who should skip* to reduce bounce from mismatched intent.

### 5) FAQ / PAA pattern seen repeatedly
Recurring questions across pages:
- Is 15 bar enough, or do I need 20 bar?
- Can I make latte/cappuccino with these machines?
- Do I need a separate grinder?
- How often do I descale/clean?
- Are pod machines “real espresso”?

**Template action:** lock these into mandatory FAQ coverage for this cluster.

## Intent gaps competitors leave open (ranking opportunity)

1. **Weak “true espresso vs convenience” framing**
   - Most pages mix pod systems, moka-style options, and pump espresso without clear expectation-setting.

2. **Pressure-bar confusion not resolved clearly**
   - Many list 15 vs 20 bar but do not explain extraction quality impact in plain language.

3. **Hidden cost blind spot**
   - Little coverage of accessory costs (tamper, pitcher, grinder, descaler), which affects real budget fit.

4. **Beginner workflow gap**
   - Few pages explain first-week usability (dial-in difficulty, preheat time, cleanup frequency).
