## Extracted structure patterns

### 1) Title angle patterns
Common winning angle stack:
- `Best ... Under $100` primary phrase at front.
- Freshness token: year/month update (`2026`, `Updated: March 2026`).
- Value framing over luxury framing ("budget picks", "great value", "don’t suck").

What to mirror:
- Keep exact budget in title (`under $100`) + current year.
- Add one proof/value modifier (`tested`, `great value`, `budget picks`).

### 2) Section order patterns
Most recurring order across ranking listicles:
1. Fast intro with budget reassurance
2. Quick winners / summary anchors
3. Comparison table near top
4. Product blocks (usually 8–12 picks)
5. Buying guide (feature education)
6. FAQ block

Notes:
- Pages that surface the table early reduce scroll-to-decision time.
- High performers often split “best overall / best value / best compact / best programmable”.

### 3) Comparison table style patterns
Observed repeated columns/themes:
- Product
- Best for
- Cup capacity
- Programmable timer (Yes/No)
- Carafe type (glass/thermal)
- Brew controls (strength, pause-and-serve)
- Footprint/size
- Price

Formatting pattern:
- Lightweight scan table before long review blocks.
- Binary/short labels outperform long prose in cells.

### 4) FAQ / PAA-style patterns
Recurring FAQ intent themes across competitors:
- What is the best drip coffee maker under $100?
- Can I get programmable features under $100?
- Is thermal carafe possible in this budget?
- How easy is cleaning/maintenance?
- How long do budget models last?
- Brew speed/time-to-cup questions

Related-search cues in SERP also push:
- “best cheap drip coffee maker”
- “fastest drip coffee maker”
- Brand-led intent (Bunn, Bonavita)

### 5) Intent gaps to exploit
Gaps repeated across current rankings:
1. **Drip-only ambiguity**: many lists mix in single-serve or even non-drip picks.
2. **Daily-friction blind spot**: weak coverage of cleanup cadence, reservoir refill cadence, and real brew time.
3. **Ownership-cost blind spot**: almost no explicit mention of filters, descaling cost/frequency, and expected lifespan.
4. **Accessory spam**: some pages include cleaner tablets and non-machine products, hurting buyer trust.

Actionable differentiation:
- Keep page strictly drip-machine-only.
- Add “workflow realism” and “12-month cost” fields.
- Add “avoid list contamination” rule (no cleaners/accessories as ranked picks).
