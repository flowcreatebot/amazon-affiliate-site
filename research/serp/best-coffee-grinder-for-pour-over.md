## Actionable SERP patterns

### 1) Title angle patterns
- Dominant framing: `Best X for Y (Year)` + list count (`5`, `7`, `11`).
- Frequent trust token: `Top Picks`, `Reviews`, `Tested`, `Compared`.
- Angle split seen in winners:
  - **Performance-first**: grind consistency/clarity for V60/Chemex.
  - **Budget-fit**: explicit under-$100 / $100–$200 / premium tradeoffs.
  - **Workflow realism**: retention, static, grind speed, hand effort.

### 2) Common section order across ranking pages
1. Short intro + quick recommendation hook
2. Top picks / winner cards (before long prose)
3. Comparison table near top
4. Individual product mini-reviews
5. Buying guide (burr type, grind range, consistency, UX)
6. FAQ + final verdict

Observation: pages skipping an early winner block/table are usually weaker in scannability.

### 3) Comparison table style patterns
- Most pages keep a **single fold-visible table** before product deep dives.
- Common columns: product, best for, grinder type/burr, key specs, price.
- Stronger pages add practical columns (not just specs):
  - grind consistency for medium-coarse ranges
  - fines/sludge risk
  - retention/static behavior
  - workflow effort/speed
- Missing opportunity on many competitors: explicit brew-method fit row (V60 vs Kalita vs Chemex).

### 4) FAQ / PAA pattern extraction
Recurring question themes:
- What grind size is best for pour over?
- Conical vs flat burr for pour over?
- Manual or electric grinder for pour over?
- Do I need a gooseneck + scale before upgrading grinder?
- How often should I clean/descale/calibrate grinder?

Low-quality pages have generic FAQ unrelated to pour-over workflows; this is a clear quality gap to beat.

### 5) Intent gaps we can exploit
- **Method-specific cup outcome gap:** SERP rarely maps grinder picks to V60/Kalita/Chemex cup profile.
- **Workflow-time gap:** almost no pages quantify time/effort for 20g morning brew.
- **Retention/static truth gap:** frequently mentioned but rarely compared consistently across picks.
- **Cross-intent linking gap:** few pages route users to companion guides (kettle/scale/ratio) at decision points.

## Recommended page execution for this cluster
- Lead with winner cards + table within first 25% of page.
- Force cup-profile language (`clarity/body/sediment`) in each product block.
- Include one practical workflow line per pick (dose time, mess/static, adjustment friction).
- Add tightly scoped FAQ matching pour-over PAA intent only.
- Add internal links to kettle, scale, and ratio guides at “next purchase” moments.
