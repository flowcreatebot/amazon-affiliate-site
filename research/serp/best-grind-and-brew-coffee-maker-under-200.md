## Actionable structure patterns

### 1) Title angle patterns that rank
- Dominant pattern: **"Best X Under $200 (Year)"** with list size token (`5`, `10`, `12`).
- High-frequency modifiers: `Buyer’s Guide`, `Top Picks`, `Comparison`.
- Weakness seen: many pages target generic "coffee makers under $200" instead of **true grind-and-brew intent**.

**Template implication:** keep exact keyword front-loaded and add intent qualifier: `with grinder` / `grind and brew`.

### 2) Common section order
Observed winning order across most pages:
1. Short intro + budget framing
2. Quick picks/winner strip
3. Comparison table (or pseudo-table cards)
4. Individual product mini-reviews
5. Buying guide
6. FAQ

Where pages are weak, they skip real grinder workflow details and only discuss generic drip-machine features.

### 3) Comparison table style patterns
- Best-performing utility pages use a **single scan-first table near the top**.
- Typical fields: product, best for, capacity, programmability, price.
- Missing fields (opportunity): grinder type, bean-to-cup time, chute cleanup frequency, compatibility (beans-only vs beans+pods).

**Template implication:** force practical workflow columns competitors omit.

### 4) FAQ / PAA pattern
Recurring question intents:
- Are grind-and-brew machines worth it vs separate grinder + brewer?
- Can I use pre-ground coffee too?
- Burr vs blade in built-in grinder systems?
- How often should grinder chute/basket be cleaned?
- Can anything under $200 make coffee shop-quality flavor?

Many pages include weak or generic FAQ blocks; strong opportunity to publish direct, specific answers.

### 5) Intent gaps we can exploit
- **Intent contamination:** several SERP results are not grind-and-brew specific.
- **Workflow realism gap:** little detail on noise, retention, morning routine friction, and cleanup cadence.
- **Compatibility gap:** very little clarity on beans-only vs dual-mode vs pod compatibility.
- **Budget-tier clarity gap:** almost no one gives explicit best picks by <$120 / $120–$160 / $160–$200.
