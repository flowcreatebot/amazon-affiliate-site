How to choose under $200

  • Prioritize the portafilter and basket quality: this affects extraction more than fancy displays.
  • Buy a separate grinder if possible: pair your machine with a burr grinder for far better shots.
  • Look for serviceable parts: replaceable baskets and gaskets matter for long-term value.

Which model should you buy?

Choose the Stilosa if you’re learning and want manual control. Choose Café Barista if your main drink is a quick latte. Choose Gevi if counter space is your hard constraint.

FAQ

Can you get real espresso under $200?

Yes, but consistency depends heavily on your grinder and puck prep. Expect better results with fresh beans and a burr grinder.

Is a built-in grinder required?

No. In this budget, separate machines usually perform better than combo units.
