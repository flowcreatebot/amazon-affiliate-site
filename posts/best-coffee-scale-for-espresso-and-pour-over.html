What matters in a coffee scale

  • Response speed: Slow lag makes espresso yield targets painful and hurts pour control.
  • Button layout: Physical buttons are often easier than touch controls around steam and splashes.
  • Stability: If weight drifts while brewing, recipes become impossible to repeat.
  • Size: Espresso trays need compact scales; pour over setups can use larger platforms.

Which scale should you buy?

For mostly pour over, the Hario V60 Drip Scale remains a reliable default. For espresso plus occasional filter coffee, the TIMEMORE Black Mirror Nano gives a faster, more flexible workflow. Frequently asked questions

Do you need a coffee scale for espresso?

Yes. Espresso quality depends on dose and yield consistency. A scale is the fastest way to make shots repeatable.

Can I use any kitchen scale for pour over?

You can, but many kitchen scales are too slow to respond. A coffee scale with a responsive readout and timer makes dialing recipes easier.
