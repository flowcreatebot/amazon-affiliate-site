# Style Guide: Buying Guide

## Purpose & Reader Intent

The reader is early in their buying journey. They do not know which product to buy yet — they might not even know what features matter. They want to understand the category, learn what to look for, and figure out their budget before browsing specific products.

## Word Range

**Target: 1,400–1,800 words**

Educational content without individual product reviews. Covers types, key factors, budget tiers, and common mistakes. Links to roundup pages for specific product recommendations.

## Structure Rhythm

1. **Lead** (1-2 sentences) — frame what the reader will learn and why it matters
2. **Types comparison** (table + 1 paragraph per type) — what kinds exist and who each suits
3. **Key factors** (1-2 paragraphs per factor) — what to look for when shopping
4. **Budget tiers** (table) — what you get at each price point
5. **Common mistakes** (bulleted list, 1-2 sentences each) — pitfalls specific to this category
6. **Featured picks** (links to roundups) — "For our specific picks, see [roundup page]"
7. **FAQ** (min 4 items, 2-4 sentences each)

## Section-Specific Guidance

### Types Comparison

**Density:** Table for the overview, then 1 paragraph per type
**What belongs here:** The key differences between types and who each type suits. Use a table for the factual comparison, then a short paragraph per type adding opinion.
**What to cut:** Full product recommendations (those belong in roundups). Technical deep-dives on each type.

### Key Factors

**Density:** 1-2 paragraphs per factor
**What belongs here:** What the factor is, why it matters to the reader, and how to evaluate it when shopping. Use concrete examples.
**What to cut:** Factors that only matter to experts. Factors already covered in the types comparison.

**Ideal example:**
> **Burr type matters more than brand.** Flat burrs produce more uniform grounds but cost more and run louder. Conical burrs are quieter and cheaper but can be less consistent at espresso-fine settings. For most home brewers doing drip or pour-over, conical burrs are fine. If espresso is your main goal, flat burrs start to make sense above $300.

### Budget Tiers

**Density:** Table format preferred
**What belongs here:** What the reader gets at each price point. Be specific about what features appear and disappear at each tier.
**What to cut:** Prose paragraphs restating the table data. Specific product names (link to roundups instead).

### Common Mistakes

**Density:** 3-5 bullet points, 1-2 sentences each
**What belongs here:** Category-specific mistakes. "Buying a filter grinder for espresso." "Choosing by hopper size when you buy small bags."
**What to cut:** Generic shopping advice ("read reviews", "compare prices").

## Pre-Submission Checklist

- [ ] Types comparison uses a table for overview + short prose per type
- [ ] Key factors focus on what the reader should evaluate, not technical deep-dives
- [ ] Budget tiers in table form, not prose
- [ ] Links to roundup pages for specific product picks
- [ ] No individual product reviews (those belong in money pages)
- [ ] Word count is within 1,400-1,800
