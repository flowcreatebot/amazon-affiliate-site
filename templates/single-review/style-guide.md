# Style Guide: Single Review

## Purpose & Reader Intent

The reader is seriously considering buying this specific product and wants to know if it is worth the money. They want a verdict, an honest assessment of strengths and weaknesses, and to know what alternatives exist if this one is not right for them.

## Word Range

**Target: 1,800–2,200 words**

One product examined in depth across performance areas, plus verdict, specs, alternatives, and FAQ. The ProductCard, verdict box, and ProsCons components handle structured data, so prose should focus on real-world usage and honest assessment.

## Structure Rhythm

1. **Lead** (1-2 sentences) — state the verdict immediately
2. **Verdict detail** (1-2 paragraphs) — expand on who this is for and who should skip it
3. **Specs table** — technical specs in table form, not prose
4. **Performance deep-dive** (min 3 subsections, 1-2 paragraphs each) — real-world usage observations
5. **Alternatives** (2-3 paragraphs) — name specific alternatives and when they win
6. **FAQ** (min 4 items, 2-4 sentences each)

## Section-Specific Guidance

### Lead Paragraph

**Density:** 1-2 sentences
**What belongs here:** The verdict. Is it worth buying? For whom?
**What to cut:** Product history, brand background, unboxing narrative.

### Verdict Detail

**Density:** 1-2 paragraphs
**What belongs here:** Who this product is perfect for, who should avoid it, and the one thing that surprised you about it.
**What to cut:** Restating the ProsCons list in sentence form.

### Specs Table

**Density:** Table only, no prose wrapper
**What belongs here:** Technical specifications in a clean table. No opinion in this section.
**What to cut:** Prose paragraphs explaining what each spec means. Save that for performance sections.

### Performance Deep-Dive

**Density:** 1-2 paragraphs per subsection (min 3 subsections)
**What belongs here:** How the product performs in specific real-world scenarios. Sensory descriptions. Honest comparisons to alternatives at similar price points.
**What to cut:** Repeating specs from the table. Generic praise without specific observations.

**Ideal example:**
> The Encore ESP holds its grind setting well, but there is a catch. When you switch from espresso to pour-over and back, you sometimes land one click off from where you were. It is not a dealbreaker — just something to watch for if you bounce between brew methods daily.

**Why this works:** Specific observation, honest caveat, framed for the reader's actual workflow.

### Alternatives

**Density:** 2-3 paragraphs total
**What belongs here:** Name 2-3 specific alternatives. For each, state one reason someone might choose it over the reviewed product.
**What to cut:** Full mini-reviews of each alternative. Just state the differentiator and link to the relevant page.

## Pre-Submission Checklist

- [ ] Verdict stated in first 2 sentences, not buried at the end
- [ ] Specs in table form, not prose paragraphs
- [ ] Performance sections describe real-world usage, not restated specs
- [ ] ProsCons data not duplicated in verdict detail prose
- [ ] Alternatives name specific products with specific differentiators
- [ ] Word count is within 1,800-2,200
