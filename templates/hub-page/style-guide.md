# Style Guide: Hub / Category Page

## Purpose & Reader Intent

The reader is browsing a category. They want to quickly find the right guide for their situation. This is a navigation page, not an editorial page.

## Word Range

**Target: 300–500 words**

Brief category introduction, then a card grid linking to all pages in the category. Minimal prose.

## Structure Rhythm

1. **Category introduction** (2-3 sentences) — what this category covers and who it is for
2. **Subcategory card grid** — auto-generated from props, no prose needed
3. **Extra content** (optional, 1-2 paragraphs) — only if the category needs context that cards cannot convey

## Section-Specific Guidance

### Category Introduction

**Density:** 2-3 sentences
**What belongs here:** What the category covers, who it is for, and what kind of guides the reader will find here.
**What to cut:** Everything else. No buying advice, no product recommendations, no SEO filler.

### Extra Content

**Density:** 0-2 paragraphs (optional)
**What belongs here:** Only if the category has context that the card grid cannot convey. For example, explaining the difference between two subcategories that might confuse readers.
**What to cut:** Content that belongs on a buying guide or roundup page.

## Pre-Submission Checklist

- [ ] Introduction is 2-3 sentences, not a full article
- [ ] Card grid handles navigation — prose does not duplicate it
- [ ] No product recommendations or buying advice
- [ ] Word count is within 300-500
