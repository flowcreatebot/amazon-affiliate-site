Buying Guide Section (.buying-guide-section)

How to Choose the Right Coffee Grinder

The single biggest upgrade for any home brewer is switching from pre-ground to freshly ground coffee. But not all grinders are equal — blade grinders produce inconsistent particle sizes that lead to uneven extraction.

Look for conical or flat burr grinders with at least 15 grind settings. For pour-over, prioritize grind consistency. For espresso, you need stepless (infinite) micro-adjustment.

FAQ Accordion (.faq-accordion)

What grind size should I use for pour over?

Medium-fine, roughly the texture of table salt. For a V60, go slightly finer than for a Chemex. Adjust based on your total brew time — aim for 2:30 to 3:30.

How often should I clean my burr grinder?

Brush out retained grounds after every use. Deep clean with grinder cleaning tablets every 2-4 weeks, depending on how oily your beans are. Dark roasts leave more residue.

Is a hand grinder worth it for espresso?

Yes, if you brew 1-2 cups daily and want precision without the cost of an electric espresso grinder. Models like the 1Zpresso JX-Pro and Timemore Chestnut X offer espresso-capable burr geometry under $150.
